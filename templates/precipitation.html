National Rainfall Index (NRI)

Definition: An indicator, developed by The Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO), which represents the quality of the crop growing season. The NRI yearly results take into consideration the precipitation that year, the average precipitation over the period 1986-2000, the seasonality of the main crop-growing season (distinguishing between northern and southern hemispheres), and what areas of the country are wetter.
